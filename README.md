# FED_Manufacturing_Forecast-Advance-Financial-Model
A dynamic financial model built for FD Manufacturing Company to forecast revenue, costs, and profitability within a manufacturing environment. The model includes 3-statement projections, break-even analysis, CAPEX planning, and sensitivity analysis, enabling scenario testing and data-driven strategic decision-making
Integrated Financial Model
Project Overview

This project is a fully integrated, three-statement financial model developed for FD Manufacturing Company, a manufacturing business. The model leverages historical financial performance to build a structured, assumption-driven forecasting framework used to evaluate profitability, capital structure, liquidity, and long-term financial sustainability.

The model is designed following best practices in financial modeling, with clear separation between inputs, calculations, and outputs to ensure auditability and scalability.

Financial Modeling Methodology
Historical Analysis & Assumption Building

Historical financial statements were analyzed to extract key operational and financial drivers, including:

Revenue growth trends

Gross margin stability

Operating expense ratios

Working capital turnover metrics

Capital intensity ratios

These drivers formed the basis for forward-looking assumptions embedded in the forecast model.

Revenue & Operating Forecast

Revenue was projected using historical CAGR and operational capacity assumptions. The model allows for scenario adjustments based on:

Volume growth

Pricing sensitivity

Margin compression/expansion

Cost of Sales (COGS) was modeled as a function of revenue using historical gross margin benchmarks, incorporating production cost behavior typical of manufacturing operations.

Operating expenses were forecasted using a mix of fixed and variable cost assumptions.

Working Capital Modeling

Working capital components were forecasted using turnover ratios:

Accounts Receivable (DSO-based projection)

Inventory (DIO-based projection)

Accounts Payable (DPO-based projection)

Changes in net working capital dynamically flow into the cash flow statement, ensuring accurate liquidity forecasting.

Capital Expenditure & Depreciation

CAPEX was projected based on capacity expansion and asset replacement assumptions. A fixed asset roll-forward schedule was constructed to:

Track gross PP&E

Accumulate depreciation

Calculate net book value

Depreciation is linked directly to the income statement and balance sheet.

Debt & Capital Structure

A structured debt schedule was built to model:

Opening balance

New borrowings

Principal repayments

Interest expense (linked to average debt balance)

The model dynamically captures leverage ratios and interest coverage metrics.

Equity accounts reflect retained earnings accumulation and financing assumptions.

Tax Modeling

Corporate tax expense is calculated based on pre-tax income, incorporating effective tax rate assumptions. The tax impact flows through net income and retained earnings.

Integrated 3-Statement Framework

The model integrates:

Income Statement

Cash Flow Statement (Indirect Method)

Balance Sheet

All statements are fully linked and balanced, ensuring structural integrity and real-time recalculation when assumptions are modified.

Analytical Outputs

The model supports:

Profitability analysis

Free Cash Flow generation

Leverage assessment

Liquidity monitoring

Scenario and sensitivity testing
